ML Research isn't accessible

Machine Learning is like an attractive deep-dark black hole….beautiful, unpredictable and intimidating - all, in equal measures.

What makes it even worse is that research papers in machine learning aren’t optimized to distill knowledge but to get the results out, quickly and narrowly - aimed only at a very specific audience i.e. the experts thus making the field far more intimidating to those are looking to newcomers including artists, musicians and many more who are looking to use machine learning methods in their fields or explore new applications

Need of the hour

M.L. will touch possibly every field by 2030 and has been replacing conventional software engineering. Higher adoption, more jobs. However, Higher Competition = Higher Barrier to Entry.

Thus, usual requirements to enter the job market include a deep understanding of the field, code project implementations, first author publications at top venues, blog posts, among others…

MLNerdie

A community effort to distill machine learning research in a bid to make machine learning accessible to everyone.

Collaborative Learning

MLnerdie provides students a platform to showcase their work and ask questions that are easier to ask among peers than in class and lectures.

Adding Dimensions To Diversity

MLnerdie serves as a platform to promote diversity in tech and especially guiding and helping people with non technical background to start their career in ML.
